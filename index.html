Nomad's Compass — Interactive PRD
Aaron S George

Story — Aditi's Trip

A solo traveler in Ladakh, stuck with patchy internet. Needs offline access to itinerary, maps, contacts, and notes.
Problem:

When Aditi loses signal, her critical travel info becomes inaccessible. Existing apps either require constant connectivity or scatter data across services.

Solution:

Nomad's Compass — an offline-first travel assistant that caches tickets, lightweight maps, essential phrases, and a journal that syncs when online.

Product Requirements (PRD)

Audience: Travelers with limited internet • Goal: Offline-first trip reliability

Key Features

  • Offline Trip Vault (tickets & bookings)
  • Offline Map Packs (small, local tiles)
  • Quick Delta Syncs
  • Essentials (SOS, phrases, cached currency)
  • Journal & Expense Log

Metrics & Non-Goals

Metrics: 80% core features offline, NPS >45, fast sync.

Non-Goals: Real-time turn-by-turn navigation, social features.

User Flows

  1. Pre-trip: App suggests downloads based on itinerary.
  2. During trip offline: View tickets, map pins, add notes/expenses.
  3. Back online: Delta sync pushes journal/expense updates.

Wireframe

Three phone mockups: Home, Offline Map, Journal

Teardown

Quick product critique and opportunities

Strengths

  • Offline-first focus solves a real pain
  • Simple, focused MVP
  • Builds user trust (safety and reliability)

Risks & Mitigations

  • Users forget to download —> Add reminders and auto-suggest packs
  • Storage/accuracy tradeoff for maps —> tiered map packs
  • Competition can copy —> build P2P sharing & offline ML
